Backward Substitution

\Mat{A} \Vec{x} = \Vec{b} \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} & \dots & a_{1n} \\ 0 & a_{22} & \dots & a_{2n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \dots & a_{mn} \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ \vdots \\ x_{m} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} b_{1} \\ b_{2} \\ \vdots \\ b_{m} \end{bmatrix} writing out the above, &a_{11} x_{1} + a_{12} x_{2} + \dots + a_{1n} x_{n} = b_{1} \\ &a_{22} x_{2} + \dots + a_{2n} x_{n} = b_{2} \\ &\qquad\qquad\qquad\vdots \\ &a_{mn} x_{n} = b_{n} and rearranging to solve for $\Vec{x}$, x_{1} &= (b_{1} - a_{12} x_{2} - \dots - a_{1n} x_{n}) / a_{11} \\ x_{2} &= (b_{2} - a_{22} x_{3} - \dots - a_{2n} x_{n}) / a_{22} \\ &\qquad\qquad\qquad\vdots \\ x_{m} &= b_{m} / a_{mn} or more generally, \boxed{ x_{i} = \dfrac{b_{i} - \sum_{j=i+1}^{1} a_{ij} x_{i}}{a_{ii}} \quad \text{where} \; i = n, n - 1, \cdots, 1 }.
