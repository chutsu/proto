Riemannian Geometry

Special Orthogonal Group $\SO{3}$

Special Orthogonal Group $\SO{3}$ describes the group of 3D rotation matrices and it is formally defined as $\SO{3} \dot{=} \{ \rot \in \real^{3 \times 3} : \rot^{\transpose} \rot = \I, \Det{\rot} = 1 \}$ . The group operation is the usual matrix multiplication, and the inverse is the matrix transpose. The group $\SO{3}$ also forms a smooth manifold. The tangent space to the manifold (at the identity) is denoted $\so{3}$, which is also called the Lie algebra and coincides with the space of $3 \times 3$ skew symmetric matrices. \angvel \in \real^{3}, \enspace \Skew{\angvel} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -\omega_{3} & \omega_{2} \\ \omega_{3} & 0 & -\omega_{1} \\ -\omega_{2} & \omega_{1} & 0 \end{bmatrix}

\text{Exp}: \real^{3} \rightarrow \SO{3} ; \enspace \boldsymbol{\phi} \rightarrow \text{exp}(\Skew{\boldsymbol{\phi}}) \\ \text{Log}: \SO{3} \rightarrow \real^{3} ; \enspace \rot \rightarrow \Skew{\text{log}(\rot)} Exponential Map

The exponential map (at the identity) $\text{exp}: \mathfrak{so}(3) \rightarrow \SO{3}$ associates an element of the Lie Algebra to a rotation: \text{exp}(\Skew{\boldsymbol{\phi}}) = \I + \dfrac{\sin(|| \boldsymbol{\phi} ||)} {|| \boldsymbol{\phi} ||} \Skew{\boldsymbol{\phi}} + \dfrac{1 - \cos(|| \boldsymbol{\phi} ||)} {|| \boldsymbol{\phi} ||^{2}} \Skew{\boldsymbol{\phi}}^{2}

Logarithmic Map

The logarithmic map (at the identity) associates rotation matrix $\rot \in \SO{3}$ to a skew symmetric matrix: \log(\rot) = \dfrac{\psi \cdot (\rot - \rot^{\transpose})}{2 \sin(\psi)}, \enspace \psi = \cos^{-1} \left( \dfrac{\Trace{\rot) - 1}}{2} \right)
